TYPES OF MENTAL HEALTH

Anxiety

A conditions in which you have anxiety that does not go away and can get worse over time. The symptoms can interfere with daily activities such as job performance, schoolwork, and relationships.

Depression

A mood disorder that causes a persistent feeling of sadness and loss of interest. Also called major depressive disorder or clinical depression, it affects how you feel, think and behave and can lead to a variety of emotional and physical problems.

Loneliness

Distressing experience that occurs when a person’s social relationships are perceived by that person to be less in quantity, and especially in quality, than desired.

Stress

Feeling of emotional or physical tension that can come from any event or thought that makes you feel frustrated, angry, or nervous it is your body's reaction to a challenge or demand.

Mental Health & Wellness

As soon as a mental disorder is identified, it needs to be treated in order to stop it from getting significantly worse. Early mental health diagnosis is related to a person's level of mental healthiness. Anyone exhibiting abnormal behaviour should get immediate treatment from a psychologist so they can be properly diagnosed with the type of mental illness they are experiencing. One-on-one interactions and therapy sessions can be used to accomplish this. The WHO highlighted that improper diagnosis and treatment particularly if it occurs at a late stage can result in a variety of illnesses.
